# Climate Inequality: Who Emits vs Who Suffers

## Problem
High-emission countries are not always the ones most vulnerable to climate change. This project quantifies that imbalance.

## Objective
Identify countries with the highest climate risk relative to their CO₂ contribution.

## Dataset
- Our World in Data (CO₂)
- ND-GAIN Index (Vulnerability & Readiness)

## Methodology
- Data cleaning & reshaping (wide → long)
- Merging datasets
- Feature engineering (Climate Inequality Index)
- Aggregation (2020–2023)
- Modeling (Random Forest)

## Key Insight
Countries with low emissions often face disproportionately high vulnerability.

## Tools
Python, Pandas, NumPy, Seaborn, Plotly, Scikit-learn

## Result
- Ranked countries by inequality
- Identified high-risk, low-emission nations
